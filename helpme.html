What course are you considering? +
You need to know what course you are considering and whether it is suitable for you.
If you don't know what course to pick look at the course catalogue to help make a decision.
When you know what course to pick email your adviser of studies to find out if it is a suitable choice.
Are you adding or swapping? +
If you are adding a course it is usually much simpler so long as it doesn't conflict with another course.
If it doesn't conflict then you can most likely do it on MyCampus. If it does conflict then read \"Who will change your courses\"
Are you in the Add/Drop period? +
The add/drop period exists to give students time to try out courses and still be able to change without falling too far behind, therefore changing courses outside the add/drop period could result in a lot of extra work for you - especially if you have missed assessment for the course you wish to change to.
Changing courses is still possible however you should certainly discuss it with your adviser to ensure that you make the best decision for you.
Who will Change your courses You or the College Office? +
Though you can change courses yourself in almost all situations you should goto the SSO first.
The SSO can then tell you if they can solve the problem or if you need to go to the college office.
However going to the SSO is only necessary when you are going to drop a course (this includes dropping a course to pick another course up and any time where you will drop a course), this is because they can add a course before dropping one which means you will never get stuck without a course.
Getting advice on your proposed changes, are you confident? +
Any time you are considering changing courses it is always recommended you discuss this with your adviser as they really are best placed to answer questions and help you decide what is right for you.
